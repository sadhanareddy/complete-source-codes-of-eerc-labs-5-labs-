Introduction:
An orifice plate is a device used for measuring the rate of fluid flow. It uses the same principle as a Venturi nozzle, namely Bernoulli's principle which states that there is a relationship between the pressure of the fluid and the velocity of the fluid. When the velocity increases, the pressure decreases and vice versa.

Theory:
Orifice is an opening provided either on one side of the wall or at a bottom of a tank. They are of different shapes like circular, rectangular, square, triangular etc. The liquid particles approach the orifice from all sides & discharging liquid comes out through the orifice like a free jet. The jet continues to converge beyond the orifice till the streamlines are parallel. The section of jet is then of minimum area & is known as venacontracta. Beyond this section it starts expanding due to viscous resistance offered by the atmosphere & partly due to inertia of the fluid particles. Generally venacontracta is d/2 (d is diameter of the orifice) distance from the orifice. The streamlines here are parallel to each other and perpendicular to the plane of orifice. The contraction and expansion of the jet causes loss of energy and therefore it is necessary to know the characteristics of a jet .
Cd= Actual discharge (Qac)/Theoretical discharge (Qth)
Cv= Actual velocity (Vac)/Theoretical velocity (Vth)
Cc= Area of jet at venacontracta (Av)/Area of orifice (Ao)
These three coefficients can be related by Cd= Cc x Cv
Actual discharge is measured by volumetric measurement
Theoretical discharge is given by Qth=Ao√(2gH)
Ao = area of orifice
H = Head causing the flow
